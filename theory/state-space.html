The general form of a state-space model can be written as two functions. $\( \begin{aligned} & \dot{\mathbf{x}}(t)=\mathbf{f}(t, x(t), u(t)) \\ & \mathbf{y}(t)=\mathbf{h}(t, x(t), u(t)) \end{aligned} \)$

The first is the state equation and the latter is the output equation. If the function \(f(\cdot, \cdot, \cdot)\) is a linear combination of states and inputs then the equations can be written in matrix notation. The \(u(t)\) argument to the functions can be dropped if the system is unforced (i.e., it has no inputs).

Linear#

The most general state-space representation of a linear system with \(p\) inputs, \(q\) outputs and \(n\) state variables is written in the following form:[14] $\( \begin{aligned} & \dot{\mathbf{x}}(t)=\mathbf{A}(t) \mathbf{x}(t)+\mathbf{B}(t) \mathbf{u}(t) \\ & \mathbf{y}(t)=\mathbf{C}(t) \mathbf{x}(t)+\mathbf{D}(t) \mathbf{u}(t) \end{aligned} \)$ where:

  • \(\mathbf{x}(\cdot)\) is called the “state vector”, \(\mathbf{x}(t) \in \mathbb{R}^n ;\)

  • \(\mathbf{y}(\cdot)\) is called the “output vector”, \(\mathbf{y}(t) \in \mathbb{R}^q\);

  • \(\mathbf{u}(\cdot)\) is called the “input (or control) vector”, \(\mathbf{u}(t) \in \mathbb{R}^p\);

  • \(\mathbf{A}(\cdot)\) is the “state (or system) matrix”, \(\operatorname{dim}[\mathbf{A}(\cdot)]=n \times n\),

  • \(\mathbf{B}(\cdot)\) is the “input matrix”, \(\operatorname{dim}[\mathbf{B}(\cdot)]=n \times p\),

  • \(\mathbf{C}(\cdot)\) is the “output matrix”, \(\operatorname{dim}[\mathbf{C}(\cdot)]=q \times n\),

  • \(\mathbf{D}(\cdot)\) is the “feedthrough (or feedforward) matrix” (in cases where the system model does not have a direct feedthrough, \(\mathbf{D}(\cdot)\) is the zero matrix), \(\operatorname{dim}[\mathbf{D}(\cdot)]=q \times p\), $\( \dot{\mathbf{x}}(t):=\frac{d}{d t} \mathbf{x}(t) . \)$

For the homogeneous problem:

\[\begin{split} \begin{gathered} \underbrace{\left(\begin{array}{cc} \mathbf{0} & \mathbf{M} \\ \mathbf{M} & \mathbf{C} \end{array}\right)}_{\mathbf{A}_B} \underbrace{\left(\begin{array}{c} \ddot{\mathbf{q}} \\ \dot{\mathbf{q}} \end{array}\right)}_{\dot{\mathbf{u}}}+\underbrace{\left(\begin{array}{cc} -\mathbf{M} & \mathbf{0} \\ \mathbf{0} & \mathbf{K} \end{array}\right)}_{-\mathbf{A}_A} \underbrace{\left(\begin{array}{c} \dot{\mathbf{q}} \\ \mathbf{q} \end{array}\right)}_{\mathbf{u}}=\underbrace{\left(\begin{array}{l} \mathbf{0} \\ \mathbf{0} \end{array}\right)}_{\mathbf{0}} \\ \Longrightarrow \dot{\mathbf{u}}=\mathbf{A u} \quad \text { where } \mathbf{A}=\mathbf{A}_B^{-1} \mathbf{A}_A \end{gathered} \end{split}\]
