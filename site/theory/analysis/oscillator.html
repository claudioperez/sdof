Consider the damped harmonic oscillator with input given by an external driving force \(f(t)\),

\[ m\ddot{u} + c\dot{u} + k u = f(t) \]
\[ \ddot{u} - 2\alpha \dot{u}+\omega_0^2 u=f \]
\[\begin{split} \begin{aligned} \mathrm{L} &= m\partial^2 + c \partial + k \\ &= \partial^2 + c/m \partial + \omega^2 \end{aligned} \end{split}\]

\(c/m = 2\gamma = 2 \omega \zeta\)

\(\gamma=\zeta\omega = c/(2m)\)

Transition#

\[ m\ddot{u} + c\dot{u} + k u = f(t) \]

Let \(\mathbf{x} = (u, \dot{u})\),

\[\begin{split} \begin{aligned} \dot{\mathbf{x}}(t) = \begin{pmatrix} \dot{u} \\ \ddot{u} \end{pmatrix} &= \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -\frac{k}{m} & - \frac{c}{m} \end{bmatrix} \mathbf{x}(t) + \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ \frac{1}{m} \end{bmatrix} f(t) \\ &=\mathbf{A}\mathbf{x}(t) + \mathbf{f}(t) \end{aligned} \end{split}\]

The state space representation of the homogeneous problem is: $\( \left[\begin{array}{l} \dot{u} \\ \ddot{u} \end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{cc} 0 & 1 \\ -\frac{k}{m} & -\frac{c}{m} \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l} u \\ \dot{u} \end{array}\right] = \underbrace{ \left[\begin{array}{cc} 0 & 1 \\ -\omega_n^2 & -2 \omega_n \end{array}\right]}_{\triangleq \mathbf{A}}\left[\begin{array}{l} u \\ \dot{u} \end{array}\right] \)\( An eigendecomposition of \)\mathbf{A}\( follows in the standard manner by requiring \)\det\left(\mathbf{A} - \lambda\mathbf{1}\right) = 0\( , furnishing the following characteristic polynomial with roots \)\lambda_i\(: \)\( \begin{aligned} \lambda\left(\lambda+\frac{c}{m}\right)+\frac{k}{m} &=0 \\ \lambda\left(\lambda-2 \xi \omega_n\right)+\omega_n^2 &=0 \\ \lambda^2+2 \xi \omega_n \lambda+\omega_n^2 &=0 \\ \end{aligned} \quad \implies \quad \begin{aligned} \lambda&=-2 \xi \omega_n \pm \frac{\sqrt{4 \xi_n^2 \omega_n^2-4 \omega_n^2}}{2} \\ &=-\xi \omega_n \pm \omega_n^2 \sqrt{\xi^2-1} \\ &=\boxed{-\xi \omega_n \pm i \omega_n \sqrt{1-\xi^2}} \end{aligned} \)\( where \)\omega_n=\sqrt{k/m}\( and \)\zeta=\frac{c}{2 \sqrt{m k}}\( The eigendecomposition is then: \)\( \begin{aligned} \mathbf{A} &= \bm{\Phi}\bm{\Lambda}\bm{\Phi}^{-1} \\ &= \left[\begin{array}{cc} 1 & 1 \\ \alpha+\beta i & \alpha-\beta i \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{cc} \alpha+\beta i & \\ & \alpha-\beta i \end{array}\right] \frac{1}{-2 \beta i}\left[\begin{array}{cc} \alpha-\beta i & -1 \\ -\alpha-\beta i & 1 \end{array}\right] \\ &= \frac{1}{-2 \beta i}\begin{bmatrix} 0 & -(\alpha+\beta i) + (\alpha-\beta i) \\ \left(\alpha^2+\beta^2\right) 2\beta i & -(\alpha+\beta i) (\alpha+\beta i)+(\alpha-\beta i) (\alpha-\beta i) \end{bmatrix} \end{aligned} \)\( where \)\alpha=-\zeta \omega_n\( and \)\beta = \omega_n \sqrt{1-\xi^2}\( so that \)\lambda = \alpha \pm \beta i\(. \)$ \begin{aligned} e^{\mathbf{A}t} &= \frac{1}{-2 \beta i}\left[\begin{array}{cc} 1 & 1 \ \alpha+\beta i & \alpha-\beta i \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{cc} e^{\left(\alpha+\beta i\right) t} & \ & e^{\left(\alpha-\beta i\right) t} \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{cc} \alpha-\beta i & -1 \ -\alpha-\beta i & 1 \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l} u_0 \ \dot{u}_0 \end{array}\right] \

&= \frac{1}{-2 \beta_1}\left[\begin{array}{cc} 1 & 1 \ \alpha+\beta i & \alpha-\beta i \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{cc} \left(\alpha-\beta i\right) e^{\left(\alpha+\beta i\right) t} & -e^{(\alpha+\beta i) t} \ \left(-\alpha-\beta i\right) e^{(\alpha-\beta i) t} & e^{\left(\alpha-\beta i\right) t} \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l} u_0 \ \dot{u}_0 \end{array}\right] \

&= \frac{1}{-2 \beta i}\left[\begin{array}{cc} (\alpha-\beta i) e^{(\alpha+\beta i) t}-(\alpha+\beta i) e^{(\alpha-\beta i) t} & -e^{(\alpha+\beta i) t}+e^{(\alpha-\beta i) t} \ \left(\alpha^2+\beta^2\right) e^{(\alpha+\beta i) t}-\left(\alpha^2+\beta^2\right) e^{(\alpha-\beta i) t} & -(\alpha+\beta i) e^{(\alpha+\beta i) t}+(\alpha-\beta i) e^{(\alpha-\beta i) t} \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{l} u_0 \ \dot{u}_0 \end{array}\right] \

&= \frac{1}{-2 \beta i} e^{\alpha t}\left[\begin{array}{ll} (\alpha-\beta i) e^{\beta i t}-(\alpha+\beta i) e^{-\beta i t} & -\left(e^{\beta i t}-e^{-\beta i t}\right) \ \left(\alpha^2+\beta^2\right)\left(e^{\beta i t}-e^{-\beta i t}\right) & -(\alpha+\beta i) e^{\beta i t}+(\alpha-\beta i) e^{-\beta i t} \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l} u_0 \ \dot{u}_0 \end{array}\right] \

&= e^{\alpha t}\left[\begin{array}{cc} \frac{-\alpha}{2 \beta i}\left(e^{\beta i t}-e^{-\beta i}\right)+\frac{1}{2}\left(e^{\beta i t}+e^{-\beta i t}\right) & \frac{1}{2 \beta i}\left(e^{\beta i t}-e^{-\beta i t}\right) \ \frac{\alpha^2 + \beta^2}{-2 \beta i}\left(e^{\beta i t}-e^{-\beta i t}\right) & \frac{\alpha}{2 \beta i}\left(e^{\beta i t}-e^{-\beta i t}\right)+\frac{1}{2}\left(e^{\beta t}+e^{-\beta i}\right) \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l} u_0 \ \dot{u}_0 \end{array}\right] \

&= e^{\alpha t}\left[\begin{array}{cc} \frac{-\alpha}{\beta} \sin \beta t+\cos \beta t & \frac{1}{\beta} \sin \beta t \ \frac{-\left(\alpha^2+\beta^2\right)}{\beta} \sin \beta t & \frac{\alpha}{\beta} \sin \beta t+\cos \beta t \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l} u_0 \ \dot{u}_0 \end{array}\right] \end{aligned} $$

It follows that $\( \mathbf{x}(t) = \bm{\chi}(t, 0)\mathbf{x}(0) + \int_0^t \bm{\chi}(t,\tau)\mathbf{B}\mathbf{f}(\tau)\, d\tau \)\( where \)\( \bm{\chi}(t, \tau) = e^{\alpha (t-\tau)}\left[\begin{array}{cc} \frac{-\alpha}{\beta} \sin (\beta (t-\tau))+\cos (\beta (t-\tau)) & \frac{1}{\beta} \sin (\beta (t-\tau)) \\ \frac{-\left(\alpha^2+\beta^2\right)}{\beta} \sin (\beta (t-\tau)) & \frac{\alpha}{\beta} \sin (\beta (t-\tau))+\cos (\beta (t-\tau)) \end{array}\right] \)$

Remarks

  • Note that if \(\zeta=0\), then \(\alpha = 0\) and \(\beta = \omega_n\), and \(\bm{\Phi}\) above reduces to the classical rotation matrix: $\( \bm{\Phi}(t, 0) = \left[\begin{array}{cc} \cos \beta t & \frac{1}{\beta} \sin \beta t \\ -\beta \sin \beta t & \cos \beta t \end{array}\right] \)$

  • at \(t=\tau\) for any \(\tau\), \(\bm{\chi}(\tau, \tau) = \bm{1}\)

  • Expanding furnishes: $\( \begin{aligned} \chi_{11}(t_i+\Delta t, t_i) &=e^{-\zeta \omega \Delta t_i}\left(\frac{\zeta}{\sqrt{1-\zeta^2}} \sin \omega_d \Delta t_i+\cos \omega_d \Delta t_i\right) \\ \chi_{12}(t_i+\Delta t, t_i) &=\frac{e^{-\zeta \omega \Delta t_i}}{\omega_d} \sin \omega_d \Delta t_i \\ \chi_{21}(t_i+\Delta t, t_i) &=-\frac{\omega}{\sqrt{1-\zeta^2}} e^{-\zeta \omega \Delta t_i} \sin \omega_d \Delta t_i \\ \chi_{22}(t_i+\Delta t, t_i) &=e^{-\zeta \omega \Delta t_i}\left(\cos \omega_d \Delta t_i-\frac{\zeta}{\sqrt{1-\zeta^2}} \sin \omega_d \Delta t_i\right) \end{aligned} \)$

Consider a linear excitation on the interval \(t_i \leq t \leq t_{i+1}\): $\( \ddot{u}+2 \zeta \omega_n \dot{u}+\omega_n^2 u=-a_i-\frac{\Delta a_i}{\Delta t}\left(t-t_i\right) \)\( where \)\( \Delta t=t_{i+1}-t_i \quad\text{ and }\quad \Delta a_i=a_{i+1}-a_i . \)\( The general solution is given by: \)\( \begin{gathered} u=e^{-\zeta \omega_n\left(t-t_i\right)}\left[C_1 \sin \omega_d\left(t-t_i\right)+C_2 \cos \omega_d\left(t-t_i\right)\right] -\frac{a_i}{\omega_n^2}+\frac{\Delta a_i}{\Delta t}\left(\frac{2 \zeta}{\omega_n^3} -\frac{1}{\omega_n^2} \left(t-t_i\right)\right) \end{gathered} \)\( with free parameters \)C_1\( and \)C_2\(. Setting \)u=u_i\( and \)\dot{u}=\dot{u}_i\( at \)t=t_i\( and solving for \)C_1\( and \)C_2\(, it can be shown that \)\( \begin{aligned} & C_1=\frac{1}{\omega_d}\left(\zeta \omega u_i+\dot{u}_i-\frac{2 \zeta^2-1}{\omega^2} \frac{\Delta a_i}{\Delta t}+\frac{\zeta}{\omega} a_i\right) \\ & C_2=u_i-\frac{2 \zeta}{\omega^3} \frac{\Delta a_i}{\Delta t}+\frac{a_i}{\omega^2} \end{aligned} \)\( Let \)\( \mathbf{a}_i=\left[\begin{array}{c} a_i \\ a_{i+1} \end{array}\right] \)\( \)\( \mathbf{x}_{i+1}= \bm{\chi}(t_i+\Delta t, t_i) \mathbf{x}(t_i)+ \mathbf{P} \mathbf{a}_i \)\( \)\( \begin{aligned} b_{11}&= e^{-\zeta \omega \Delta t}\left[\left(\frac{2 \zeta^2-1}{\omega^2 \Delta t}+\frac{\zeta}{\omega}\right) \frac{\sin \omega_d \Delta t}{\omega_d}+\left(\frac{2 \zeta}{\omega^3 \Delta t}+\frac{1}{\omega^2}\right) \cos \omega_d \Delta t\right] -\frac{2 \zeta}{\omega^3 \Delta t} \\ b_{12}&=-e^{-\zeta \omega \Delta t}\left[\left(\frac{2 \zeta^2-1}{\omega^2 \Delta t}\right) \frac{\sin \omega_d \Delta t}{\omega_d}+\frac{2 \zeta}{\omega^3 \Delta t} \cos \omega_d \Delta t\right] -\frac{1}{\omega^2}+\frac{2 \zeta}{\omega^3 \Delta t} \\ b_{21}&=e^{-\zeta \omega \Delta t}\left[\left(\frac{2 \zeta^2-1}{\omega^2 \Delta t}+\frac{\zeta}{\omega}\right)\left(\cos \omega_d \Delta t-\frac{\zeta}{\sqrt{1-\zeta^2}} \sin \omega_d \Delta t\right)\right. \\ & \left.-\left(\frac{2 \zeta}{\omega^3 \Delta t}+\frac{1}{\omega^2}\right)\left(\omega_d \sin \omega_d \Delta t+\zeta \omega \cos \omega_d \Delta t\right)\right] +\frac{1}{\omega^2 \Delta t} \\ b_{22}&=-e^{-\zeta \omega \Delta t}\left[\frac{2 \zeta^2-1}{\omega^2 \Delta t}\left(\cos \omega_d \Delta t-\frac{\zeta}{\sqrt{1-\zeta^2}} \sin \omega_d \Delta t\right)\right. \\ &\left.-\frac{2 \zeta}{\omega^3 \Delta t}\left(\omega_d \sin \omega_d \Delta t+\zeta \omega \cos \omega_d \Delta t\right)\right]-\frac{1}{\omega^2 \Delta t} \end{aligned} \)$
